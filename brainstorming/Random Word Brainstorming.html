The Situation:
How to catch a murderer.

Your random word is:   script

Idea:  Screenplay
Sheriff finds details of the murder in a suspects screenplay.
 
Idea:  Salesmen
A salesman is the next victim.
 
Idea:  Telemarketing
Telemarketers trick people into giving up personal information about themselves.
 
Idea:  Handwriting
the Sheriff matches handwriting on a note at the crime scene or on a victim to the murderer. Perhaps ghostly handwriting.
 
Idea:  Cursive
The murderer always writes in block print to disguise his handwriting. When forced to write in cursive, the Sheriff can recognize the handwriting.
 
Idea:  Plan
The writing the Sheriff compares is a plan to complete several murders in a ritual.
 
Idea:  Plot
The ritual is the plot of a book.
 
Idea:  Greek
The book is about ancient Greek Gods.
 
Idea:  Uncial
The block caps of the murderer are medieval Uncial caps.
 
Idea:  Pens
The murderer used a fountain pen to make marks in the victims that looked like vampire bites.
 
Idea:  Quills
A feather from the quill is matched to one of a stuffed bird the murderer keeps on display.
 
Idea:  Signature
The signature is the bit of cursive that gives the murderer away when mostly he writes in Uncial block letters.
 
Idea:  Unique
It is the unique form of one of his letters, perhaps an 's' or an 'f' that is the tell-tale.
